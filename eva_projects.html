Intention

Design a branching choice system where all 6 endings feel reachable and earned through natural play — no single "correct" path, no endings that require a guide.

Process

Mapped 47 choice nodes in Google Sheets, assigning weighted values per ending path. Ran 30+ full playthroughs to verify each ending was accessible before submission.

Challenge

Playtesting showed 80% of players landed on the same 2 endings. Early Act 1 choices were so heavily weighted they locked the player's path before the midpoint — the other 4 endings were effectively unreachable.

Solution

Redistributed point weights across Act 1 — reduced early-choice influence, shifted narrative tension to mid-game decisions instead. Rebalanced until all 6 endings registered in natural playthroughs.

Outcome

All 6 endings became accessible through normal play. Players consistently cited that "my choices actually mattered" — the exact feeling the narrative was built to create. The game reached 500K+ organic downloads with zero paid acquisition, and was recognized as Best Narrative Game at IGDX 2022.

What was delivered
  • Full branching choice system — 47 nodes, 6 distinct endings, all verified accessible through 30+ playthroughs
  • Act 1 point weight rebalance — identified and resolved the path lock-in causing 80% of players to hit the same two endings
  • Monetization strategy reviewed and implemented with player trust as the primary constraint
  • Community built across X, Instagram, Facebook, and Discord from zero — supporting 500K+ organic downloads
⬡ Best Narrative Game — IGDX 2022 · Finalist LevelUP KL 2021 · Best Team — Gameloft Game Design Connect 3, 2023

Intention

Make wait periods between matches feel engaging, not empty. Build a trait-compatibility system where no single character combination dominates the outcome.

Process

Designed 100+ random events using a structured content framework (setup → reaction → outcome). Built a full 30+ trait interaction matrix in Google Sheets with positive, neutral, and negative scores per pairing.

Challenge

Two simultaneous problems: (1) Players were skipping events in early testing — engagement was near zero. (2) The "Adventurer" trait was generating successful matches at 3× the expected rate, breaking the balance.

Solution

(1) Restructured event triggers — early events now feature already-familiar characters, increasing engagement through familiarity. (2) Rebuilt the scoring formula to separate base compatibility from trait bonuses. Rebalanced 40+ combinations across 3 test cycles until all traits normalized.

Outcome

Post-fix playtests showed players actively reading events rather than dismissing them. The wait period became a feature, not a problem. No single trait dominated at ship. Matchmaker Agency launched on PC and Nintendo Switch with a fully balanced system across 30+ traits, 20+ items, and 8 locations — all documented in Google Sheets for team reference.

What was delivered
  • 100+ random events — designed, written, paced, and balanced for variety across the full game session
  • Full trait compatibility matrix — 30+ traits, positive/neutral/negative scores per pairing, balanced across 3 test cycles
  • Complete item and location economy — no dominant strategies at ship
  • Special character trigger system — specced with unlock conditions and rarity logic, handed off for programmer implementation
  • All systems fully documented in Google Sheets for whole-team reference
⬡ Best Game Designer — Gameloft Game Design Connect 3, 2023 · Available on Steam and Nintendo Switch

Intention

Adapt a beloved Indonesian comic IP into a mobile visual novel that satisfies both existing fans and new players — without distorting source material or losing the characters that fans care about.

Process

Served as the bridge between IP and mechanics. Designed the dress-up system ruleset, built and balanced the point/progression system in Google Sheets, and reviewed all adapted scenes against the source comic for fidelity.

Challenge

Working inside a licensed IP means the story is fixed. Every mechanic had to create player agency and engagement without touching the protected narrative. No dramatic restructuring. No character motivation changes.

Solution

Focused design energy entirely on the spaces the IP didn't define — between-scene moments, the customization layer, and reward pacing. Designed player agency where the story allowed it without pulling against the source material.

Outcome

Delivered a complete mobile game for a major licensed IP from a major Indonesian media company. The dress-up mechanic and point system were fully designed, tested, and shipped within the constraints of the licensed property. All adapted scenes were verified against source material before production lock.

What was delivered
  • Dress-up mechanic — full ruleset, unlock logic, edge-case QA, and constraint documentation
  • Point and progression system — designed and balanced for full game flow
  • Story fidelity review — all adapted scenes verified against source comic material
  • Complete design delivered within licensed IP constraints for Bumi Langit, one of Indonesia's leading media companies
